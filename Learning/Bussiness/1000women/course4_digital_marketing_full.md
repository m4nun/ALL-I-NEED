By identifying the channels in which your audience is most active, you can align your content with your customers' needs at the digital touchpoint where they are most active. **Owned media** refers to the digital channels and content that are in your control. That means you have full control of the messages of your digital marketing campaign, and of the story you want to tell about your product or service. This can include your own websites, your own brand pages on social media, email newsletters and your own mobile apps.

## 7. Using Earned Media

You will now explore earned media and how this can be used in your digital marketing strategy.

You will learn about different types of earned media, how to incentivize earned media effectively, the role that influencers can play and how to choose a suitable influencer to increase interest in your product and attract your product personas.

### What is Earned Media?

Earned media refers to any publicity a company receives from unpaid promotional efforts. It is often generated by opinion leaders and other third party sources like journalists, bloggers, and influencers.

Earned media can come in many forms, such as:
- reviews left by previous customers on shopping platforms
- detailed video reviews
- social media posts on the product/service.

### How Can You Incentivize Earned Media?

One of the most common forms of earned media is **word-of-mouth (WOM) marketing** which is any verbal, informal, person-to-person communication. Earned media is an important aspect of an integrated digital marketing campaign, as 91% of customers regularly read online reviews and 20%-50% of all purchasing decisions are driven by WOM marketing. However, it isn't easy to control what customers say about your brand, product or service.

To solve the issue, you can incentivize WOM marketing. One of the ways of doing this is through **influencer marketing**. Influencer marketing means leveraging individuals who have influence over potential buyers and creating marketing activities around these individuals to drive a brand message to the larger market.

In addition to monetary payment, there are many other ways of incentivizing influencers to provide favorable WOM. In the UK, payment can also be in-kind, including any type of incentive from gifts to free products and services such as hotel stays or free trips.

### How Can Influencers Help?

Marketers can have control over the content which influencers put out. This can include everything from the pre-approval of content, specific use of hashtags, timing and frequency of posts to specifying key themes and messages including images and styles. In short, you can incentivize influencers to amplify the messages you have generated in your owned and paid media.

It is important to be transparent and get your chosen influencer to disclose these incentives accordingly. When they have received payment for the WOM and you have control, the post should be labelled as an **"Advertisement"** or **"Ad"**. However, if you didn't have control over the content, the post should be labelled as a **"Sponsorship"**.

Customers could respond negatively once they've learnt that the influencer was incentivized to provide a positive review. One way of mitigating this is to allow the influencers to provide a balanced review, containing both positive and negative aspects. Consumers also react positively to incentivized reviews if they believe that the influencers are driven by their inner desires and passions. It could be beneficial to pick influencers who are genuinely interested in the product or service that you are offering.

### How Do You Choose an Influencer?

When you are choosing an influencer, one of the things to consider is the number of followers they have. A 'nano-influencer' can have up to 1,000 followers while 'mega influencers' can have more than 1 million followers.

While mega influencers are great at raising awareness of your brand/product/service, influencers with smaller followings can often establish a closer and more meaningful connection with their audience. Therefore, they are more likely to appear to be genuinely interested in a particular product, so their messages could be less affected by incentivization. This means that influencers with smaller followers could be deemed more credible and thus more useful at the later stages of the customer journey.

As an example, let's return to our marketing persona, Customer 2 (the camera enthusiast). As a camera enthusiast, Customer 2 'follows' an influencer who is a professional photographer online. Considering the role that influencers can play in the customer journey, it might be worthwhile to incentivize such influencers, especially if they are genuinely interested in your product and can provide detailed reviews. You could even offer discount codes for them to disseminate to help push customers like Customer 2 further along their customer journey and purchase your product.

By making the most of earned marketing approaches such as WOM marketing and identifying appropriate influencers to attract customers, you can use earned media as part of a successful integrated digital marketing campaign.

## 8. Exercise 2: Using Viral Marketing

Another common form of earned media is **viral marketing** which is the process of getting customers to pass along a company's marketing messages to friends, family, and colleagues. This often comes in the form of videos, ads, and other marketing content that are so infectious that customers will seek it out or pass it along to friends.

A common trait of all viral content is an **emotional connection** with their audience. Viral ads often evoke surprise and other emotions, capturing the imagination of their target personas. **Humor** also works well, especially if it's related to the product.

You can also promote content sharing by embedding social media links in your content and connecting with your audience in the comments.

Unsurprisingly, many marketers would love their content to go viral as it generates a great deal of awareness and reach at a very low cost. Furthermore, as the content is often shared by friends and family, it also lends the persuasive message some credibility.

However, it is hard to predict whether marketing content will go viral, and it is important to be aware that sometimes marketing content can go viral for the wrong reasons and generate a considerable reaction as a result of poor content.

## 9. Monitoring the Success of Your Digital Marketing Campaign

Now that you have learned how to use owned, paid, and earned media in a digital marketing campaign, you come to the final key consideration of your digital marketing campaign: how to measure its success.

### Owned Media (Webpages)

You have learned how to optimize your website to market your brand, product or service using owned media. Specifically, you explored mapping the relevant keywords to the webpages based on your various customer journey objectives. Now let's go through the **key performance indicators (KPIs)** to measure its effectiveness.

**Owned media KPIs** include the following:
- average position
- page views
- bounce rate.

**Average position** indicates how well your webpage is positioned on search engine results pages. Since your aim is to rank high on the results page, the smaller the number, the better.

**Page views** displays the number of views you have had for a particular page on your website. You can also find out when and which events drove page views. These times and events can serve as potential touchpoints to reach out to more customers.

**Bounce rate** is the percentage of visitors who navigate away after viewing one page.

A high bounce rate can mean:
- your content does not match your target customer's needs
- the page has low quality content
- visitors found the information they needed quickly and exited the page.

For reference, an excellent bounce rate is between 26 to 40% while an average is between 41 and 55%.

You could expect the bounce rates to differ across different type of webpages. When your webpage is awareness-driven and aimed at customers like Customer 1 (hobbyist), who is searching for more information, you will want to achieve an excellent bounce rate as you want Customer 1 to stay on your website viewing different pages to push them along the customer journey funnel.

On the other hand, for Customer 2 (camera enthusiast), who is at the later stages of the customer journey, and debating between cameras, a conversion-driven webpage with a clear call-to-action like "Buy Now" and product comparisons could provide Customer 2 with all the information they need on one page, boosting the bounce rate. In other words, a higher bounce rate might not always be a bad sign. It depends on your customer journey objectives.

### Paid Media (PPC and Social Media Ads)

Earlier, you learned about how to use paid media in your digital marketing campaign. Specifically, you explored how to use PPC ads to promote your marketing content. What are the KPIs you can use to gauge how effective these ads were?

**Paid media KPIs** include the following:
- impressions
- clicks
- click-through-rate
- cost per click
- cost
- conversions
- conversion ratio.

**Impressions** refer to the number of times your ad is displayed on the search engine results pages or social media platform. This KPI gives you an idea of the amount of traffic that the ad is receiving. Put differently, it gives you an idea on the amount of awareness you are generating.

While impressions give you an idea if the ad is appearing on the search engine results page or social media platform, it does not mean that your target customer sees it. For that, you can rely on clicks.

**Clicks** are the number of times the PPC ad is clicked on. This KPI indicates the level of engagement the ad is receiving.

**Click-through-rate (CTR)** is the number of impressions that attracted a click. Just like clicks, this KPI also gives you an idea about the level of engagement the ad is receiving. If the CTR is low, it suggests that the ad content does not really match the target customer's needs and needs to be revised.

**Cost per click (CPC)** is the average cost per click on the ad.

**Cost** is the budget spent on the ad. Recall that you do not pay for PPC ads unless they are clicked on. Thus, the budget spent is the CPC x the number of clicks. For example, if the ad's CPC is $0.83 and was clicked on 2,101 times, then its cost amounts to $0.83 x 2,101 = $1,742.

**Conversions** are the number of purchases or sign-ups. This KPI is a good indication of the quality of your traffic.

**Conversion ratio** is the percentage of visitors who converted. For example, if 2,101 people clicked on your ad and 140 people purchased the product, then your conversion ratio is 6.66%.

### Which Measures Should You Select?

Ultimately, which KPIs you refer to should match your objectives.

If your PPC ad was aimed at Customer 1 (hobbyist) and you just wanted to generate awareness of your product, then KPIs like impressions, clicks and CTR will serve as good indicators.

If your ad was aimed at Customer 2 (camera enthusiast) and you intended to push him further down the funnel to convert and purchase your product, conversions and conversion-rates will serve as better indicators of your ad's success.

## 11. Using Social Listening

**Social listening** is the monitoring of your brand's social media channels for any customer feedback and direct mentions of your brand, or discussions regarding specific keywords, topics, competitors, or industries. This is then followed by an analysis to gain insights and action on these opportunities.

In other words, social listening means tracking your social media platforms for any mentions or conversations related to your brand.

You can do this in two steps:
1. **Monitor** your social media channels for mentions of your brand, competitors, products, and any keywords relevant for your business.
2. **Analyze** the information and use these insights to shape or optimize your marketing strategy.

### Why Does It Matter?

Social listening provides you with insights into the following areas:
- campaign and sentiment analysis
- content research
- influencer recognition.
